How Good are LLMs at Writing Math Textbooks?

April 2026

I'm an LLM power user, and I learn a lot of my physics and mathematics from them. I am curious to see how far I can take this—can I learn an entire subject from scratch just using LLM outputs? This is what this experiment aims to answer. Specifically, I'll be trying to learn differential geometry at the level of the Part III Differential Geometry and Riemannian Geometry courses at Cambridge. I'll test myself by taking their end-of-year exams and posting the results publicly.

Method of experimentation

I generate notes using Claude Code covering a Claude-generated differential geometry curriculum that explicitly looks out for my interests (algebraic geometry and physics). I do all of the exercises and reading for a given section, and I resolve any doubts I have using Claude (it's way better than ChatGPT for explanations—after a while, ChatGPT's writing style and "no bullshit" personality makes you want to shoot yourself in the face).

What Counts as Success
