About Me

Academics and Interests

I am a Junior in physics and mathematics at Stony Brook University (graduating May 2026).

My physics interests mainly lie in quantum field theory, specifically its non-perturbative and on-shell formulations. I think these vectors are useful ones for solving some of the biggest problems in physics today. There is also a sort of experimental duality that comes with these subjects — non-perturbative QFT finds many examples in condensed matter physics, while on-shell QFT has manifold connections with cosmology.

I also have interests in pure mathematics, and I think modern physics will require new and more advanced mathematical techniques for its implementation. Specifically, the Geometric Langlands program in physics is interesting to me, although I have very little knowledge of it as of now. The work with the positive Grassmannian a la Nima Arkani-Hamed also seems quite interesting.

My current main interest — and probably career path — is machine learning and artificial intelligence. I am new to the field, but I find it incredibly interesting, and I think it's the most important technology of the 21st century. I am interested in creating better learning algorithms for models, as well as new model architectures. I'm also extremely interested in using AI for theoretical physics research.
