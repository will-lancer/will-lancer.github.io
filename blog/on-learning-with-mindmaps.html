On Learning with Mind-maps

January 2026

My process starts with structure, not details. Before reading deeply, I map the rough shape of the material so I know what ideas connect and what ideas are upstream.

The loop

  1. Skim the resource and draft a broad mind-map first.
  2. Read deeply and only add high-value updates to the map.
  3. When understanding changes, rewrite the map from scratch.
  4. Run free-recall sessions before learning the next batch of content.

Why this works for me

It separates thinking from transcription. If writing becomes the default activity, learning quality drops. The map acts as a model of the subject rather than a long transcript of the source.

I do not treat this as universal advice. It is just what has worked after a lot of trial and error.
